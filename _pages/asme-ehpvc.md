## Morgan State university's Inaugural ASME E- Human Powered Vehicle Team

This project was a first for the university in both technical challenge and operational in the sense that this was entirely student led. As a student I was serving as the Technical Advisor and Project Manager for this competition. From the ground up, I led a team of 10 students to learn how to CAD, Program, Manufacture, and ultimately create a functional prototype vehicle. I learn to leverage all connections and resources I had outside of the university for creating this vehicle would not be able to be done on campus due to work hours and manufacturing limitations. I learned to ask for support from friends, supporters, and donors, balancing their expectations and the teams outcome as invested stakeholders. It was by far the most fun and fulfilling project I have worked on in my undergraduate studies. 

The objective of Morgan State’s ASME Student Section was to create a vehicle suitable for most 
members of the general public. Especially commuters and students.  The vehicle should be able 
to reach top speed with little effort, aided by a motor and the individual’s attributes. The vehicle 
should have a comfortable range for both functional and leisure travel. Equipped with a 
multitude of safety and comfort features to help protect the rider from the elements and 
hazards. Ultimately, this team is heavily student-led, with students learning CAD, programming, 
and manufacturing on the fly. We look forward to the feedback and learning experiences from 
the competition, so we know what bar we need to aim for the next time we compete.

## Bike concept and design 
The vehicle design concept our group chose to follow is a recumbent tricycle. This is because 
the objective of a recumbent bike is to provide a comfortable, low-physical stress, long-term 
ride for our drivers. We also considered significant health benefits for our drivers. Such as their 
strength and physical endurance. The vehicle concept we heavily looked into was a recumbent 
tadpole tricycle with RWD(rear wheel drive). The benefits we saw in bike design across many 
reports related to this competition were stability and speed. From previous competition 
designs, we observed that we considered a welded roll-over protection system (RPS) with a 
welded connection to the frame. Heavily citing the competition rules for the requirements of 
the RPS and the frame for building. As well as considering the vehicle dynamics and how we 
want the vehicle to move.
